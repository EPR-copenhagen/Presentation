Born-Oppenheimer Approximation

Born-Oppenheimer Approximation

Simplify the problem by splitting of problem into two parts: Electronic and Nuclear

\[ m_{nuclei} >> m_{electron} \]

\[ \hat{H}_{B.O} = -\frac{1}{2} \sum_{i=1}^{N} \nabla_i^2 - 0 + \sum_{i=1}^{N}\sum_{A=1}^{M} \frac{Z_A}{r_{iA}} + \sum_{i=1}^{N}\sum_{j>1}^{N} \frac{1}{r_{ij}} + constant \qquad(3)\]

Now, only electronic Hamiltonian remains

From wavefunction to electron density

Probability desnity \[ |\psi_{electron (r_1 \dots, r_N)}|^2 = |\psi_{electron (r_1 \dots, r_N)}^* \psi_{electron (r_1 \dots, r_N)}| \qquad(4)\]

Electron desnity \[ n(\vec{r}) = 2 \Sigma_{i} \psi_{i}^* (\vec{r}) \psi_{i}(\vec{r}) \]

Problem is reduced from 3N variables to 3 variables

Hohenberg-Kohn Theorems (1964)

First HK Theorem

For any system of interacting particles in an external potential \(V_{ext}(\vec{r})\), the potential \(V_{ext}(\vec{r})\) is determined uniquely by the ground state particle density \(n_0(r)\).

\[ E_{ground} = E[n(r)] \qquad(5)\]

Second HK Theorem

The density that minimizes the energy functional is the exact ground state density (variational principle)

\[ E_{o} \leq E_{trial} = \int \psi_{trial}^* \hat{H} \psi_{trial} \qquad(6)\]

Energy Functional

\[ E[\rho] = T[\rho] + E_{Ne}[\rho] + E_{ee}[\rho] \qquad(7)\]

\[ E[\rho] = T[\rho] + E_{Ne}[\rho] + E_{ee}^{classical}[\rho] + E_{XC}[\rho] \qquad(8)\]

The explicit form of \(T[\rho]\) and \(E_{XC}\) is the major challenge of DFT

\[ E_{KS}[\rho] = \overbrace{T_s[\rho] + E_{Ne}[\rho] + E_{ee}^{classical}[\rho]}^\textit{known functionals} + \overbrace{E_{ee}^{XC}[\rho]}^\textit{unknown functionals} \qquad(9)\]

\(E_{XC}\) includes:

  • Exchange and correlation energy
  • Correction of Kinetic energy term \(\Delta T_{\rho}[\rho]\)

  • Tight Binding

  • Translation images of each other
